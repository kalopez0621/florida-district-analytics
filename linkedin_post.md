I built a dashboard.

Not for a class. Not for a tutorial. I wanted to prove I can work with real K-12 assessment data the way districts actually use it.

It covers three Miami-Dade middle schools using Florida's FAST assessment framework. The data is synthetic, but the rules are real. FLDOE proficiency cuts by grade level, learning gains logic, Pts4LG targets, lowest 25% calculations, MS acceleration. All of it pulled from the state's published tables.

Four tabs:
- School Performance (all 7 School Grades components)
- Subgroup Equity (ELL, ESE, ethnicity gaps)
- Learning Gains & Growth (PM1 to PM3 trajectories)
- PM3 Predictor (bubble students closest to proficiency)

The last tab is the one I'm most proud of. It projects PM3 scores, flags students within striking distance of the proficiency cut, and models what happens to school-wide rates if you move them. That's the kind of analysis that actually changes how a school allocates resources.

Built with Python, Streamlit, Pandas, and Plotly. Live and deployed.
